Terafab consequences need a model, not just a narrative

Terafab Decision Twin turns public, declarative assumptions into auditable thermodynamic, economic, infrastructure, governance, and public-policy consequences while keeping unknown values explicit.

What stakeholders get
  • Capital risk visible before commitment.
  • Thermodynamic, infrastructure, economic, governance, and policy constraints in one frame.
  • Evidence labels separating facts, assumptions, and unknowns.

The problem

Power, water, heat, yield, cost, and legitimacy do not fail in isolation.

A terafab-scale story can sound plausible while hiding the first binding constraint. The decision twin keeps coupled risks visible before sunk-cost momentum hardens the plan.

Powerfirm capacity, load factor, cost, emissions
Heatrejection load, cooling reserve, exergy loss
Waterwithdrawal, consumption, UPW, wastewater
Valueyield, readiness, capex, opex, gate action

The trust engine

Credibility from first principles and public information

Inputs are evidence-coded before analysis begins. Public and declarative assumptions remain distinct from verified facts, and unknown values remain visible rather than being silently filled.

Evidence labels

Verified, filed or reported, user-provided, scenario assumption, stress test, unknown, confidential input, and derived output stay distinct.

Non-promotion rule

Scenario assumptions, reports, uncertainty ranges, and confidential inputs are not promoted into verified project facts.

Unknown discipline

Unknown values are not silently filled into conclusions unless the scenario explicitly allows substitution.

Restricted-source boundary

Public outputs do not redistribute private, confidential, or restricted source documents.

Stakeholder decision surfaces

One model, four stakeholder decision surfaces.

Stakeholder decision surfaces compress uncertainty, strategy, reduced-order dynamics, and validation-readiness boundaries into views that boards, investors, policymakers, and researchers can challenge without treating scenario outputs as verified data.

01Board view

Gate-failure probability, dominant gate risks, recommended phase posture.

02Investor view

Cost distribution medians and strategic conflict signals.

03Policy view

Water, wastewater, legitimacy, and public-benefit risk indicators.

04Research view

Run counts, game profiles, trajectory steps, and validation level.

How it works

Real-world scenarios in. Decision consequences out.

The model validates scenario JSON, audits evidence status, preserves unknown-value discipline, expands time steps, runs formal equation modules, and emits outputs that keep blockers and assumption boundaries visible.

  1. Validatescenario structure, evidence metadata, unknowns
  2. Computethermodynamics, economics, infrastructure, governance, public policy
  3. Gatefirm power, water, cooling, readiness, legitimacy
  4. ReportJSON, Markdown, CSV, warnings, assumptions used

Who it helps

Interpretation rule

Outputs are scenario-dependent analytical estimates derived from declared assumptions, model structure, and available public or user-supplied inputs. They must not be presented as verified operating facts without independent validation.
