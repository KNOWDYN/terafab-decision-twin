Equation architecture

Transparent equations for megafab-scale energy-water-policy simulation.

The model is designed as an extensible equation registry. It exposes the scalar and vector outputs needed to reason about power, cooling, water, exergy destruction, yield, readiness, economics, governance, policy, and uncertainty.

Equation families

Open each drawer to see the public-facing equations and their policy interpretation.

First-law power balance
E_total = Σ(P_process + P_facility + P_cooling) · Δt

Connects process loads and supporting infrastructure to site energy demand. Outputs feed grid stress and cost modules.

Entropy and exergy destruction
X_destroyed = T₀ · S_generated

Shows where useful work potential is degraded by irreversibility, heat rejection, and process inefficiency.

Water and cooling burden
W_net = W_withdrawal · (1 - reuse_fraction)

Separates withdrawal, consumption, reuse, and cooling technology assumptions for water-stressed policy analysis.

Yield and production readiness
effective_output = nominal_output · yield · readiness · qualification_factor

Prevents headline capacity from being confused with qualified, saleable output.

Thermoeconomics and real options
value = revenue - energy_cost - water_cost - yield_loss - policy_friction + option_value

Translates physical constraints into economic and strategic decision variables.

Governance and policy gates
gate_status = f(evidence, readiness, permitting, infrastructure, uncertainty)

Models whether scenario outputs should be interpreted as feasible, conditional, blocked, or uncertain.

Model flow

Scenario inputs enter the schema, evidence labels classify each assumption, equations compute physical and economic outputs, and gates determine whether the results are usable for policy interpretation.
