Model architecture

Physics, finance, readiness, and public legitimacy share one calculation path.

The package validates evidence-coded scenario JSON, expands a time axis, computes equation modules, and returns scalar, vector, and matrix outputs for decision review.

Input

Evidence-coded scenarios

Material assumptions carry value, unit, status, source reference, confidence, and notes.

Kernel

Deterministic solver

The core engine computes coupled consequences across infrastructure, operations, economics, and policy.

Output

Gate matrix

Decision gates expose blockers instead of hiding risk inside a single aggregate score.

First-principles equations

The model starts from conservation laws to build the decision surface

These eight equations are the smallest verifiable set: energy is conserved, entropy penalizes waste, materials flow through finite tools, and every constraint becomes a gate leaders can audit.

1 · Energy balance

Heat that must leave the facility

\[Q_{\mathrm{remove}}=P_{\mathrm{IT}}+P_{\mathrm{process}}+P_{\mathrm{aux}}-W_{\mathrm{useful}}-\frac{dU}{dt}\]

Derived from the first law of thermodynamics: every watt that is not stored or converted into useful work becomes heat the site must remove.

2 · Exergy loss

Irreversible work potential destroyed

\[X_{\mathrm{destroyed}}=T_0\,S_{\mathrm{gen}}\]

Derived from the second law: entropy generation converts theoretically available work into unavoidable loss, making thermal design auditable.

3 · Cooling water

Water required by heat rejection

\[\dot m_{\mathrm{water}}=\frac{Q_{\mathrm{remove}}}{c_p\,\Delta T\,\eta_{\mathrm{cooling}}}\]

Derived from sensible heat transfer: heat load, water heat capacity, allowable temperature rise, and cooling efficiency determine the flow burden.

4 · Carbon intensity

Emissions from time-varying power

\[E_{\mathrm{CO_2e}}=\int_0^T P_{\mathrm{grid}}(t)\,I_{\mathrm{grid}}(t)\,dt\]

Derived from dimensional accounting: kilowatts integrated over time become energy, then grid intensity converts energy into emissions.

5 · Tool throughput

Finite capacity of the manufacturing line

\[W_{\mathrm{starts}}=\sum_i\frac{N_i\,A_i\,T}{\tau_i}\]

Derived from rate limits: each tool family contributes available qualified time divided by its cycle time, exposing the bottleneck directly.

6 · Yield learning

Good output after defect escape

\[D_{\mathrm{good}}=W_{\mathrm{starts}}\,D_{\mathrm{wafer}}\,Y,\qquad Y=e^{-D_0A_{\mathrm{die}}}\]

Derived from Poisson defect statistics: larger die or higher defect density reduce the probability that a die survives manufacturing.

7 · Net value

Constraint-adjusted economic exposure

\[V_{\mathrm{net}}=D_{\mathrm{good}}\,p_{\mathrm{die}}-C_{\mathrm{energy}}-C_{\mathrm{water}}-C_{\mathrm{delay}}-C_{\mathrm{risk}}\]

Derived from cash-flow accounting: physical output creates value, while scarce utilities, schedule slips, and unresolved risks subtract from it.

8 · Decision gate

Go only when every critical margin is nonnegative

\[G=\min_j\left(\frac{M_j-L_j}{L_j}\right)\ge 0\]

Derived from feasibility analysis: the tightest normalized margin controls the decision, preventing one severe blocker from being hidden by averages.
