Board and policy use

The model translates engineering stress into choices leaders can govern.

It gives executives, policymakers, investors, and technical reviewers a shared language for constraints that usually appear in separate conversations.

Board questions

Where does the scenario become grid-constrained? Which water assumption controls feasibility? What readiness bottleneck blocks capital commitment?

Policy questions

What public infrastructure must precede scale? Which incentives should price water, emissions, delays, and legitimacy risk?

Investor questions

How do capex, opex, yield, energy price, water price, and delay risk move cost per output?

Engineering questions

Does cooling capacity cover heat after reserve? Does firm power cover modeled load? Which subsystem fails first?

Staged decisions

The highest-value answer may be build, delay, redesign, stage, contract, curtail, or abandon.

The model is valuable because it makes those options explicit before sunk-cost momentum narrows the conversation.
