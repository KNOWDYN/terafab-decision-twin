Board and policy use cases

The model translates engineering stress into decisions a board can actually govern.

It makes the hidden coupling between power, water, yield, exergy, economics, and public policy legible to executives, policymakers, investors, and technical reviewers.

Board questions

  • Where does a scenario become grid-constrained?
  • Which water or cooling assumption controls feasibility?
  • How does yield uncertainty propagate into economic exposure?
  • What staged option has the highest value under evidence limits?

Public-policy questions

  • What infrastructure must exist before a Terafab-scale commitment?
  • Which risks should be priced into incentives or permitting?
  • Where are energy-water tradeoffs most severe?
  • Which claims require independent verification before public reliance?
